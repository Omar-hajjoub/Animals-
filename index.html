Javan rhinoceros

The Javan rhinoceros Rhinoceros sondaicus is a critically endangered species of rhinoceros native to the island of Java in Indonesia. It is the smallest of the surviving rhinoceros species, weighing between 900 and 2,300 kilograms. The Javan rhinoceros is characterized by its grey skin, and its horns are smaller compared to those of African rhinoceros species.

Bear Grizzly

Grizzly bears, scientifically known as Ursus arctos horribilis, are a subspecies of brown bears characterized by a distinctive hump on their shoulders. They exhibit a robust build, long claws, and varying fur colors from blond to black. Inhabiting diverse ecosystems, including forests, tundra, and alpine meadows, grizzly bears are found in North America, particularly in Alaska, Montana, Wyoming, and Canada.

Polar Wolf

The Arctic wolf (Canis lupus arctos) is a subspecies of the gray wolf uniquely adapted to the harsh conditions of the Arctic tundra. Recognized by its thick white fur coat, Arctic wolves inhabit the northern regions of North America and Eurasia, surviving in icy landscapes and tundra environments.

Siberian Tiger

The Siberian tiger, also known as the Amur tiger, is the largest subspecies of tiger and one of the largest big cats in the world. Native to the forests of eastern Russia, particularly in the Amur-Ussuri region, as well as parts of China and North Korea, these tigers have a distinctive thick coat of fur ranging in color from pale orange to deep rusty red, adorned with black stripes. Their habitat includes a variety of environments such as taiga, mixed forests, and mountainous areas.
